About CeMoQu · Cerebellar Motor Quantification

Objective, web-based assessment of motor and speech function.

About

Purpose

CeMoQu is a lightweight, browser-based platform that objectively measures motor and speech features related to cerebellar disorders using a camera and microphone. It preserves established clinical scoring while adding rich, quantitative data for tracking and research.

The Problem

Ataxia causes impaired coordination and speech. It is rare, often diagnosed late, and lacks sensitive, objective outcome measures—making clinical trials and longitudinal tracking difficult.

Approach

Watch • Measure • Track — CeMoQu records video and audio, extracts numerical features (reaction time, tremor, speech intelligibility, sway, etc.), and presents them for longitudinal comparison while retaining clinical meaning.

Key Tests

Speech Disturbance: reads five sentences to assess intelligibility, rate, pitch and loudness stability. Random Target Touch: captures accuracy, reaction time, tremor and smoothness during quick target touches. Line Drawing: measures deviation and drift when tracing a path. Sitting Test: quantifies sway magnitude and frequency while sitting with arms extended.

Results & Validation

Technical consistency checks (simulated conditions) show reliable mapping of recorded behavior to expected classifications across trials. Clinical validation with clinicians and larger cohorts is the next step.

Future Directions

Expand to more SARA domains (gait, stance, fast alternating movements), calibrate with experts, and build longitudinal datasets for AI-driven biomarkers and synthetic data support.
